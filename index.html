A Due Diligence Call That Beat the Market by Two Weeks
The clearest example of forming an independent risk thesis and being right before the consensus caught up.
🏆 The Block · 2022 · One of the Block's all-time top-5 most-read stories

He flagged structural risk in UST's reserve mechanism two weeks before $45 billion evaporated

Most coverage of Terra's collapse came after the fact. Osato's didn't. Working from the mechanics of how UST maintained its dollar peg, the size of the Anchor Protocol subsidy keeping yield artificially high, and the thinness of the reserves actually backing the system, he formed a view that the structure was fragile well before the market priced that in. He published the thesis. Fourteen days later, the peg broke and the Terra ecosystem lost $45 billion in value. This is fundamental due diligence on a protocol's reserve design, a risk assessment grounded in mechanism rather than sentiment, and a thesis that was published, dated, and proven right. It is also why the story remains one of the five most-read pieces in The Block's history.

A Sourcing System Built Before It Was Asked For
His own newsroom wrote a feature about how he finds stories. The same habits generate deal flow just as well as news flow.
📋 Newsroom Feature · DL News

His editors profiled his own research method because it was distinctive enough to write about

Osato maintains a daily habit of reading governance forums across every DAO he covers, the same way a beat reporter reads committee filings. He follows official Discord servers and the unofficial ones that spring up when a team tries to control the narrative. He treats crypto Twitter as a primary source for tracking which builders, founders, and on-chain researchers are worth following closely, not as a feed to scroll. The output of that habit is a stream of early signal: which protocols are quietly in trouble, which founders are shipping something worth a second look, and which narratives are forming before they have a name. That is a sourcing pipeline. It currently produces news leads. It would produce deal leads just as easily.
